Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our federated AI platform

How do you handle devices dropping in and out during training rounds?
Federated optimization algorithms are designed to be asynchronous and faulttolerant, so each round only needs a subset of devices to respond. Late or offline clients simply join future rounds, which is critical when dealing with mobile or intermittently connected hardware.
Why is this useful for a customer or end user?
Users get smarter, more personalized features over time without feeling like all their data is being shipped to the cloud. This can mean better recommendations, faster responses, and a stronger sense of privacy and control.
Does my personal data leave the device?
No, the idea is that raw data such as texts, images, or sensor logs stay on the device. Only model updatesnumbers that describe what the model learnedare sent back.
How does this help with privacy and compliance?
Because less raw data is collected centrally, there is less risk of big data leaks and fewer worries about who can see which records. This supports "privacy by design," which many regulations and customers now expect.
What is the business advantage of doing it this way?
The business gets better models trained on more realworld data without taking on as much datastorage risk or cost. It also creates a clear privacy story that can improve user trust and make sales and compliance conversations easier.
What happens if some devices send bad or poisoned updates?
Robust aggregation and anomaly detection methods can limit the impact of malicious or low-quality clients on the global model.
